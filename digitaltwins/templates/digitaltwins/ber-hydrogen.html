BER Hydrogen Testing Facility

Organization: BluEnergy Revolution Location: Italy Domain: Hydrogen

The BER node provides a real-world testing environment for PEM electrolyzers, enabling controlled hydrogen production experiments and the generation of high-quality operational data.

Users can request custom experimental campaigns through the EnergyGuard platform, defining operating conditions such as load profiles and system behavior. The resulting datasets support the development and validation of advanced analytics and AI-based solutions for hydrogen systems, including performance optimization, predictive maintenance, and operational strategy assessment.

By combining physical assets with structured experimentation, the BER node offers reliable, data-driven insights derived from actual system operation, supporting the transition toward efficient and scalable hydrogen technologies.

What this service does

Submit a custom experiment to the BER laboratory and receive high-quality operational data from a real PEM electrolyzer. You define the operating conditions — the BER team handles the physical execution and delivers the resulting dataset to the EnergyGuard platform.

Experiment configuration options
Control mode

Choose between current-based (Ampere) or power-based (Watt) control of the electrolyzer.

Regeneration mode

Set when the hydrogen dryer regenerates: immediately when saturated, or at the next startup.

Setpoint schedule

Define a sequence of operating setpoints over time to simulate load-following behaviour or specific test profiles. Maximum experiment duration: 8 hours.

How to get started
1

Build your experiment using the visual command editor, or upload a pre-prepared JSON configuration file.

2

Review your configuration and submit the request to the BER laboratory team.

3

Once your experiment is scheduled and executed, data will be uploaded to the EnergyGuard Data Lake overnight.

After the experiment completes, you will receive a notification and can access your dataset on the platform — including key indicators such as hydrogen production rate, stack voltage and current, power consumption, system state, and dryer status.
