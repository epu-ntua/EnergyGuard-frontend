ENGREEN Node

Antrodoco Renewable Energy Community

Real-world Renewable Energy Community Testing Environment

The ENGREEN node provides a real-world testing and experimentation environment based on the Antrodoco Renewable Energy Community (REC), located in central Italy. It combines operational data from REC users and assets, real-time monitoring infrastructure, historical metering datasets, and a dedicated Digital Twin developed in collaboration with HAL.

Users can access AI-based services for photovoltaic production forecasting, anomaly detection, shared energy estimation, and REC planning. The Digital Twin enables comparison of baseline REC behaviour with forecasted, simulated, or AI-enhanced scenarios, supporting both technical validation and decision-support activities.

The node is particularly valuable for AI developers, researchers, energy SMEs, and policy makers seeking to test, validate, and demonstrate AI solutions for renewable energy communities, local energy management, and sustainability planning.

What this simulator does

Estimate how much electricity a photovoltaic plant can produce and how that production is split between energy fed into the grid and energy self-consumed on-site.

Available simulation modes
Short-term forecast

Predicts daily and hourly production for the next 1–16 days using real weather forecast data.

Historical baseline

Estimates the typical annual yield averaged over the last 5 years of observed weather.

Optimistic scenario

Estimates the theoretical maximum annual yield under ideal clear-sky conditions.

How to get started
1

Select an existing REC station or define a new hypothetical plant (panels, power, tilt, orientation).

2

Choose a consumption profile that reflects how the household typically uses energy.

3

Select your simulation type and, for short-term forecast, set the number of days ahead.

After the simulation completes, you'll see a summary of total production, energy fed into the grid, and self-consumed energy — broken down by day, hour, or month depending on the scenario.
