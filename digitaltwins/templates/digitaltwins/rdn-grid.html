Portuguese Transmission Grid

Organization: R&N Nester Location: Portugal Domain: Power Systems & Grid Stability

This node provides a high-fidelity digital twin of a large scale electricity grid with high shares of renewable energy sources (RES), inspired in the Portuguese electricity transmission grid. The purpose of this digital twin is to allow testing, evaluating and validating AI solutions to support the operation of smart grids.

Grid Specifications

The digital twin represents a network of:

  • ~3000 km of lines/cables at 400 kV
  • ~3800 km of lines/cables at 220 kV
  • ~2500 km of lines/cables at 150 kV
  • Seven interconnectors with a different control area

The full grid contains electricity generation installations of:

  • Biomass
  • Gas
  • Hydro-pump storage
  • Hydro with reservoirs and run-of-river
  • Photovoltaic
  • Wind power generation
Simulation Capabilities

The digital twin is endowed with EMT and RMS simulation capabilities.

Its models of synchronous machines, power park modules and load consumption enable the modification of injection/consumption levels across the network, while continuously assessing the impact of such changes on key system variables, namely voltage profiles and frequency behaviour.

Furthermore, the digital twin provides the ability to study the interaction with a number of intelligent electronic devices (IEDs) through hardware-in-the-loop simulations.

Hence, the digital twin of large scale electricity grid with high-shares of renewable energy sources provides realistic, physics-based operating scenarios that can be used for the training, testing and validation of AI solutions under diverse grid conditions, supporting the development of trustworthy AI applications for power system operation and control.

Automatically Configurable Use Cases

The user can automatically configure the scenarios to run the following use cases:

  • Technical Validation of Market Results
  • Voltage Control
  • Governor and Load Frequency Control

Examples of specific applications include:

  • Testing and experimentation of AI-based forecasting of renewable generation and/or load consumption
  • Voltage support coordination process
  • Assessment of the estimation of model-free voltage profiles, or operational envelopes
  • Tools for dynamic security assessment
  • Strategies to control ROCOF
  • Resilience to events resulting in frequency instability
Use Cases Requiring Manual Configuration

The following use cases cannot be configured automatically — configuration of the digital twin for these requires contact with the development team.

  • Short-circuit analysis
  • Stator transient
  • Subsynchronous resonance
  • Inverter-based control
