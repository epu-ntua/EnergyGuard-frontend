Facility

This facility represents the large-scale residential building stock of the city of Riga, mainly composed of multi-apartment buildings from different construction periods and with diverse building types and energy performance characteristics. It provides a real urban environment for evaluating AI-driven energy efficiency solutions at building and city scale. The facility is supported by data from the national EPC registry, Latvia's Construction Information Systems, municipal geospatial databases, and monitored heating consumption records.

Digital Twin

Within the EnergyGuard platform, the facility is represented through the Digital Twin of Riga's Buildings, a geospatial data-enriched visualization of the city's residential building stock, presented via an interactive map interface. Each building is enriched with EPC information, structural and audit data, continuously updated heating consumption records and city-level historical and real-time meteorological data. Users can explore, filter, and analyze building performance within the urban context. Beyond visual exploration, API access to the underlying datasets enables solution providers to integrate building data into their applications, analytics pipelines and decision support tools, supporting AI experimentation and validation in the building sector.

Use cases: Building energy performance analysis, Retrofit planning and renovation scenario simulation, Energy consumption savings estimation.

How it works

Data Sources

EPCs, energy audit data, heating energy consumption, meteorological data, State Land Dataset.

Update Frequency

Streaming heating energy consumption monitoring, combined with real-time and historical meteorological data updates.

Level of Abstraction

Building-level structured data represented through a geospatial city-scale visualisation, complemented by city-level meteorological data collected from multiple monitoring stations.
