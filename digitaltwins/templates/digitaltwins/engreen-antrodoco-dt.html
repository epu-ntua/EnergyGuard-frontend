What is this service?

The PV Production Simulator estimates how much electricity a photovoltaic plant can produce over a selected time period, and how that production splits between energy consumed on-site and energy exported to the grid.

Use it to analyse an existing station already registered in the Antrodoco REC, or to explore what a hypothetical new plant might produce based on specifications you define yourself — before any equipment is installed.

What you'll need to provide

Three quick configuration steps before running a forecast.

1
Plant configuration

Choose whether you want to simulate an existing REC station or define a new hypothetical plant. Existing stations auto-fill installed power, orientation and tilt; new plants require you to enter panel count, power rating, tilt angle and azimuth.

2
Consumption profile

Select the profile that best describes how energy is typically used at this location — day-heavy, evening-heavy, or mixed. This determines how much production is self-consumed versus fed into the grid.

3
Forecast type

Pick the kind of result you need: a short-term weather-based forecast, a historical baseline, or an optimistic clear-sky ceiling.

Available forecast types

Choose the kind of result that fits your question.

Short-term forecast

Predicts daily and hourly production for the next 1–16 days using real weather forecast data.

Historical baseline

Estimates the typical annual yield averaged over the last 5 years of observed weather.

Optimistic scenario

Estimates the theoretical maximum annual yield under ideal clear-sky conditions.

What you'll get

Once the simulation runs, you'll see a summary of total energy produced, how much was self-consumed, how much was fed into the grid, and the feed-in ratio — shown as hourly or daily charts.
