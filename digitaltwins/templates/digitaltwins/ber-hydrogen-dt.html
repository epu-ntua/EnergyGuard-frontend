Submit a custom experiment to the BER laboratory and receive high-quality operational data from a real PEM electrolyzer. You define the operating conditions; the BER team handles the physical execution and delivers the resulting dataset to the EnergyGuard platform.

Configure an experiment
One request, real operational data

This node runs experiments on a real physical asset rather than a simulation. It queues your experiment on the PEM electrolyzer at the BER laboratory and provides the measured dataset upon completion of the execution.

How it works

Upload

Upload your experiment configuration in JSON format.

Submit

Submit your request to the BER laboratory team.

Execute

The experiment runs on the real PEM system.

Results

User is notified once data is uploaded to the Data Lake.

What you'll need to provide

Control mode

Choose between current-based (Ampere) or power-based (Watt) control of the electrolyzer.

Regeneration mode

Set when the hydrogen dryer regenerates: immediately when saturated, or at the next startup.

Setpoint schedule

Define a sequence of operating setpoints over time to simulate load-following behaviour or specific test profiles. Maximum experiment duration: 8 hours.

What you'll get

System state

Dryer columns state & State of Charge (SOC). Stack voltage & current

H2 production

Measured hydrogen output of the electrolyzer over the course of the experiment.

Power consumption

Total electrical power drawn by the system during execution.
